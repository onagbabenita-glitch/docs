In VidSpark's Prompt to Video mode (Mode 3), your creative direction operates on two axes. **Style** defines the visual aesthetic — the rendering approach, color treatment, and cinematic language Veo uses to construct your scene. **Mood** defines the emotional tone — the pacing, atmosphere, and feeling the video evokes in the viewer. Together, they give you precise control over both *how your video looks* and *how it feels*.

## Video Styles

Choose a style that matches the visual world you want to build.

| Style | Aesthetic | Ideal Scenes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cinematic** | Film-quality with dramatic angles, depth of field, and color grade | Action sequences, storytelling, trailers |
| **Cartoon** | Illustrated, bright colors, flat or cel-shaded animation | Comedy, kids content, whimsical scenes |
| **Realistic** | Natural lighting, lifelike textures, resembles documentary footage | Nature, everyday life, interview-style |
| **Abstract** | Non-literal shapes, flowing colors, motion-art aesthetics | Music videos, mood pieces, experimental |
| **Sci-Fi** | Futuristic cityscapes, tech interfaces, neon lights | Space, AI, cyberpunk environments |
| **Nature** | Organic environments, wildlife, landscapes | Travel, meditation, environment themes |

## Video Moods

Mood shapes the emotional register of your video — the energy, pace, and atmosphere Veo applies to the scene.

| Mood | Emotional Tone | Best Paired With |
|---|---|---|
| **Epic** | Grand, sweeping, powerful | Cinematic, Sci-Fi, Nature |
| **Calm** | Peaceful, slow-paced, serene | Nature, Realistic, Abstract |
| **Dark** | Brooding, tense, ominous | Cinematic, Abstract, Sci-Fi |
| **Joyful** | Bright, upbeat, playful | Cartoon, Nature, Realistic |
| **Mysterious** | Enigmatic, suspenseful, haunting | Abstract, Sci-Fi, Cinematic |

## Writing Strong Scene Descriptions

Your scene description is the single most important input in Prompt to Video. Veo reads it as a director reads a script — the more clearly you set the scene, the more confidently it can render your vision.

- **Include subject + setting + action:** a complete description names *who or what* is in the scene, *where* the scene takes place, and *what is happening* — e.g., *"a lone astronaut walking across a barren red planet, dust swirling around their boots"*
- **Mention lighting conditions:** lighting is the fastest way to establish mood — *"golden hour backlight," "neon-lit night scene," "harsh midday sun," "soft overcast glow"* all dramatically shift the output
- **Add a visual quality descriptor:** phrases like *"4K," "cinematic widescreen," "drone shot," "macro close-up"* signal the production quality and framing you're aiming for
- **Keep it under 100 words for best results:** focused, well-structured prompts consistently outperform sprawling ones — Veo responds best to clear direction, not exhaustive description
