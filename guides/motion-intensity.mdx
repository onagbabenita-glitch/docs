Motion intensity is the dial that controls how much Veo AI moves the world inside your image. When you feed a still image into VidSpark's Image to Video mode (Mode 2), Veo doesn't just play it back — it brings the scene to life by inferring depth, atmosphere, and motion from the visual content. The intensity level you choose tells Veo how aggressively to apply that movement, from the quietest breath of life to full kinetic energy.

## The Three Levels

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Subtle" icon="feather">
    Barely-there motion — nearly imperceptible changes that add life without drawing attention to themselves. Expect slight atmospheric movement like drifting particles, slow breathing, a faint shimmer of light, or the gentlest sway. Best for **portraits**, **still-life scenes**, and any image where tranquility is the goal.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medium" icon="wind">
    Visible, natural motion at a realistic pace. Elements flow as they would in the real world — hair moves, water ripples, fabric shifts. This is the most versatile setting and works across a wide range of scenes. Best for **landscapes**, **general-purpose animation**, and most everyday use cases.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dynamic" icon="bolt">
    Strong, expressive movement with high energy. Expect bold camera motion, fast-moving elements, and a sense of dramatic force throughout the frame. Best for **action scenes**, **sports**, **dramatic weather**, and **abstract visuals** where energy and impact are the priority.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Choosing the Right Intensity

Selecting the right intensity comes down to three factors: your **subject**, the **mood** you want, and the **story** the video should tell.

**Consider your subject.** A portrait of a person sitting quietly calls for Subtle — Dynamic intensity applied to a still face can produce unnatural, jarring results. A wide landscape with trees and a river lends itself to Medium, where natural environmental motion feels authentic. An action shot mid-sprint or a lightning storm is where Dynamic earns its name.

**Consider your desired mood.** Intensity is a mood amplifier. Subtle creates calm and intimacy. Medium feels balanced and grounded. Dynamic creates urgency, excitement, or tension. Ask yourself: *how do I want the viewer to feel?* — and let that answer guide your intensity choice.

**Consider the video's purpose.** If you're creating a looping ambient background for a meditation app, Subtle is almost always right. If you're producing a hype reel or an action promo, Dynamic will serve you better. For social content, product showcases, or narrative storytelling, Medium is a reliable starting point.

## Motion Description Tips

Your written motion description works hand-in-hand with the intensity level. The more clearly you describe what should move and how, the more accurately Veo can fulfill your vision.

- **Be specific:** name the exact elements in the scene that should animate — *"the waterfall," "her hair," "the smoke rising from the chimney"*
- **Describe direction and speed:** *"slowly drifting upward," "whipping side to side," "gently swaying left to right"* all give Veo precise motion vectors to work with
- **Reference atmosphere:** evocative phrases like *"a gentle breeze," "crashing waves," "a flickering candle"* carry implied motion that Veo understands naturally
- **Avoid conflicting motions:** asking for *"perfectly still water with violent splashing"* in the same description can confuse the model — keep motion cues internally consistent

## Example Prompts

Use these examples as a starting point for your own motion descriptions.

| Motion Description | Intensity Level | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|
| *"Gentle ripples on the water surface"* | **Subtle** | Calm, barely-moving water reflection |
| *"Leaves rustling in a light breeze, clouds drifting slowly"* | **Medium** | Organic, natural scene with quiet life |
| *"Lightning strikes in the background, trees whipping in a storm"* | **Dynamic** | Dramatic storm-like motion across the frame |

<Note>
  The same motion description will produce different results at each intensity level. Try them all to find the best fit.
</Note>
