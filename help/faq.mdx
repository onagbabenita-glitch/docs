This page answers the most common questions from VidSpark users — covering everything from how generation works and which AI models power each mode, to tips for better prompts and what to expect when downloading your creations.

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="How long does it take to generate an image or video?">
    Typical generation takes **10–30 seconds** depending on the content type. While VidSpark is working, you'll see the loading animation:

    > *VidSpark is generating... ⚡*

    Keep the browser tab open until generation completes. Generation time may vary during high-traffic periods.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the difference between Imagen and Veo?">
    VidSpark uses two distinct Google AI models:

    - **Imagen** — Generates still images from text prompts. Used exclusively in **Mode 1 (Image Generator)**.
    - **Veo** — Creates and animates video content. Used in both **Mode 2 (Image to Video)** and **Mode 3 (Prompt to Video)**.

    Each model is purpose-built for its output type, giving you high-quality results whether you're creating artwork or motion content.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I generate a video directly from a text prompt?">
    Yes. Use **Mode 3 (Prompt to Video)** to go straight from words to motion:

    1. Describe your scene in detail.
    2. Choose a **video style** (e.g., Cinematic, Cartoon, Sci-Fi).
    3. Select a **mood** (e.g., Epic, Calm, Mysterious).
    4. Veo will generate a **5–8 second video** ready to download or regenerate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use my own image in VidSpark?">
    Yes. In **Mode 2 (Image to Video)**, you can upload any image from your device. VidSpark passes that image to Veo along with your motion description and intensity setting to produce an animated video.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What art styles are available for image generation?">
    Mode 1 (Image Generator) supports **seven art styles**:

    | Style | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Photorealistic | Lifelike, true-to-life imagery |
    | Cinematic | Film-quality composition and lighting |
    | Anime | Japanese animation-inspired visuals |
    | Oil Painting | Rich, textured brushstroke style |
    | Watercolor | Soft, translucent painted look |
    | Dark Fantasy | Moody, atmospheric fantasy art |
    | Minimalist | Clean lines, reduced color palette |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What video styles are available for Prompt to Video?">
    Mode 3 (Prompt to Video) offers **six video styles**:

    - **Cinematic** — Dramatic, film-quality motion
    - **Cartoon** — Animated, stylized visuals
    - **Realistic** — True-to-life video footage
    - **Abstract** — Non-representational, artistic motion
    - **Sci-Fi** — Futuristic, otherworldly aesthetics
    - **Nature** — Organic, natural environments
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What moods can I apply to a Prompt to Video scene?">
    Mode 3 includes **five mood options** that shape the overall tone and energy of your video:

    - **Epic** — Grand scale, powerful, and intense
    - **Calm** — Peaceful, slow, and serene
    - **Dark** — Moody, shadowy, and brooding
    - **Joyful** — Light, upbeat, and vibrant
    - **Mysterious** — Enigmatic and suspenseful
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I animate an image I generated in Mode 1?">
    After Imagen generates your image in Mode 1, click the **'Turn this into a Video →'** button that appears below the result. VidSpark will automatically pass your image into **Mode 2 (Image to Video)**, where you can add a motion description and choose an intensity level before Veo animates it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the motion description do in Image to Video mode?">
    The motion description tells Veo exactly **how elements within your image should move**. Be specific about which parts of the scene are animated and the character of their movement.

    <Tip>
      Instead of writing *"make it move"*, try something like: *"the clouds drift slowly left, leaves rustle gently in the wind, and the water ripples outward from the center."*
    </Tip>

    The more descriptive you are, the more accurately Veo can interpret your creative intent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the difference between motion intensity levels?">
    Mode 2 (Image to Video) offers three intensity settings that control the **amount of movement** in your animated video:

    | Intensity | Effect |
    |---|---|
    | **Subtle** | Barely perceptible movement — ideal for atmospheric, still-life animations |
    | **Medium** | Natural, flowing motion — a balanced default for most scenes |
    | **Dynamic** | Strong, energetic movement — best for action-oriented or dramatic content |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I regenerate a video if I don't like the result?">
    Yes, but **only in Mode 3 (Prompt to Video)**. After your video generates, click the **Regenerate** button to produce a new version using the same scene description, style, and mood inputs.

    <Note>
      The Regenerate button is not available in Mode 2 (Image to Video). To try a different result in Mode 2, click **Start Over** and re-upload your image with revised settings.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where are my downloaded files saved?">
    Downloaded images and videos are saved to your **device's default downloads folder** as determined by your browser settings. VidSpark does not store your generated content in the cloud — once you leave or reset your session, your creations are only available via the files you've downloaded.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I go back to the mode selector?">
    Click the **Start Over** button that appears after every generated output. This resets your session and returns you to the main mode selector, where you can choose between Image Generator, Image to Video, and Prompt to Video.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long are the generated videos?">
    Veo generates videos between **5 and 8 seconds** in length. Video duration is determined automatically based on your scene description and style — it cannot be manually set at this time.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
